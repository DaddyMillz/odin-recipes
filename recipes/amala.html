Amala

Description

Amala is a smooth, swallow-like Nigerian delicacy made of yam flour, cassava flour, or plantain flour, often enjoyed with rich soups and stews.

Ingridient

  • Yam flour (Elubo) - for the classic version
  • OR Cassava flour (Lafun) - for cassava amala
  • OR Plantain flour - for plantain amala
  • Water

Steps To Prepare Amala

  1. Boil water: Pour some water into a pot and bring it to a boil
  2. Add yam flour: Gradually sprinkle the yam flour (elubo) into the boiling water. Do not pour it all at once, this prevents lumps.
  3. Turn immediately: Use the wooden spatula to stir and turn quickly as you add the flour.
    Keep stirring until it forms a smooth, stretchy dough without lumps.
  4. Adjust texture: If it's too thick, add a little hot water, cover for a minute, then stir again.
    If it's too soft, sprinkle in a little more yam flour and stir well.
  5. Allow to steam: Cover the pot and leave for about 2 minutes on low heat so it cooks properly.
  6. Final stir & serve: Give it one last turn to make it smooth.
    Wrap in nylon/cling film or scoop directly into a serving plate.
